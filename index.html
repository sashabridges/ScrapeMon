Questions for Pokemon

  • How many Pokemon with two types have good speed?

    • If we classify "Good Speed" as having a speed >= 90, there are 15 Pokemon with good speed.

    We found this out by seperating the Pokemon that who have two different types.

    only_two_type = api_df[api_df["Type 2"] != "No Second Type"]

    Then we used sort_values() to sort by the Speed column.

    speed = only_two_type.sort_values(by='Speed', ascending=False)

    The Pokemon with the highest Speed stat is Wigglytuff with a Speed of 140. It is a Normal/Fairy type Pokemon, and it's pre-evolution, Jigglypuff, comes in 3rd place with a Speed of 115.

    Second place goes to Lapras, which has a Speed of 130. Lapras is a Water/Ice type Pokemon. 6 Water-type Pokemon fall in the good speed category.

    5 Flying-type Pokemon fall in the good speed category.

    With this data, we can make an educated guess that any new Water-type and Flying-type Pokemon we see are generally fast.

  • What type of Pokemon has the highest Special Attack?

    • The individual Pokemon with the highest Special Attack is Cloyster, with 180 Special Attack.

    However, what is the Pokemon type with the highest Special Attack?

    To find out, we started back at the api_df. We sorted the DateFrame by the "Special Atk" column.

    special_attack = api_df.sort_values(by='Special Atk', ascending=False) dropped = special_attack.drop(special_attack.index[20:])

    We take the top 20 Pokemon and now we will count the values.

    dropped.value_counts(['Type 1', 'Type 2'])

    The winning type of the Special Attack stat? Ground type!

    There are 4 Pokemon with the highest Special Attack, and they are all both Rock and Ground type.

    This would make it seem like Rock/Ground tied for the spot. But if you look at the data further, there are 2 Ground type Pokemon with no second type.

    There's also a weird point - there is one Ground/Rock type Pokemon, which was counted in its own category.

    The second type with the highest Special Attack is Water, followed by Rock.

  • What are the most common Pokemon abilities?

    • Figuring out the most common Pokemon abilities was pretty straight-forward. Once again value_counts() comes in handy.

    api_df.value_counts(["Ability One"]) api_df.value_counts(["Ability Two"])

    Using this, we're able to see that the most common Ability One is Chlorophyll with a count of 9. In second place is Swift Swim with 8. Third place is a four-way tie, with Run Away, Keen Eye, Poison Point, and Intimidate all having 7.

    The most common Ability Two is a tie between Sturdy and Rivalry.
